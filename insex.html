Frequently Ask Question

CWhat are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Age: Most helmets have a lifespan of about 3-5 years, even if they look fine. Check the manufacturer’s guidelines.
Visible Damage: Look for cracks, dents, or any other visible damage. If it’s been in a crash, even if it looks okay, it’s best to replace it.
Foam Condition: If the foam is compressed, crumbly, or showing signs of wear, it’s time for a new helmet.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Tires: Check for proper inflation and look for any cracks or wear. Replace if necessary.
Brakes: Test both front and rear brakes. Make sure they’re responsive and not worn down.
Chain: Clean and lubricate the chain if it’s rusty or dry. Check for any stiffness.
Gears: Shift through the gears to ensure everything is working smoothly.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Wear a Helmet: Always wear a properly fitted helmet to protect your head.
Follow Traffic Laws: Obey all traffic signals, signs, and lane markings, just like a motor vehicle
Ride Predictably: Signal your intentions with hand signals, and maintain a straight line to help others anticipate your movements.
Stay Visible: Wear bright or reflective clothing, especially at night, and use lights on your bike.
Use Bike Lanes: Whenever available, use designated bike lanes. If not, ride in the same direction as traffic.
